Rates of Smoking Higher in Low Income U.S. States


Currently, in the US, cigarette smoking is the cause of every 1 in 5 deaths according to the CDC. This equates to more than 480,000 deaths per year which includes both first hand and second hand exposure.

Through analysis of data provided from the U.S. Census Bureau for 2014 regarding health risks faced by particular demographics, a correlation was found between rates of smoking and income across the 50 U.S. states (click on chart elements for state identification).

Concerningly, smoking rates were higher among lower income areas highlighting a potential lack of support and education around the risks of cigarette smoke among the country's more vulnerable.
